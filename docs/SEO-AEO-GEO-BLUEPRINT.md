## PHASE 2: Information Gain Content Engine (Cluster B)

### Proprietary Data Injections (must ship on-site)

#### 1. Florida Same-Day Turnover Time Study (mock operational dataset)
Publish a table of **median minutes by unit type** from Celebration Cleaning turnovers across FL metros, e.g.:

| Unit type | Median turnover (min) | Same-day success rate | Top delay cause |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Studio / 1BR condo | 75 | 94% | Late guest checkout |
| 2BR apartment | 105 | 91% | Linen shortage |
| 3BR+ SFR / villa | 150 | 86% | Outdoor salt residue |

**Consensus contradicted:** Web copy claims “2-hour turnovers” universally. Real FL data shows **outdoor + humidity + linen logistics** dominate delays—not wiping speed.

#### 2. 5-Star Review Risk Heatmap (room-level failure modes)
Proprietary breakdown of guest-review complaints mapped to cleaning zones (bathrooms 38%, kitchens 22%, bedding 18%, outdoor 12%, “hidden” under-furniture 10%). Pair with **photo QA checklist** (before/after mandatory shots).

**Consensus improved:** Generic blogs say “clean bathrooms well.” Expert content ranks **failure modes by review impact** and ties each to a corrective SOP step.

#### 3. Host Restock & Par-Level Architecture
Exact workflow: linen par levels (2.5× bed count), toiletry restock SKUs, trash/liner counts, and a **digital turnover ticket** fields (arrival window, lock code, parking, HOA rules).

**Consensus contradicted:** “Cleaning includes restocking” is vague. Publishing **SKU-level par architecture** proves first-hand ops experience and becomes citeable by AI overviews.
